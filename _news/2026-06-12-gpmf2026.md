        <img src="{{ '/assets/images/gpmf2026.png' | relative_url }}" alt="GPMF 2026 Masterclass: Concurrent Engineering Programs" class="img-fluid rounded shadow">
        <p class="mt-4 text-left">
          Modelware founder and CEO, Dr. Maged Elaasar, will co-deliver a masterclass at the
          <a href="https://gpmf.world/en" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Global Project Management Forum (GPMF) 2026</a>,
          taking place September 19 to 21, 2026 in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia. The masterclass, titled
          <em>Concurrent Engineering Programs: How Great Teams Design Complex Systems Together</em>, opens the forum
          on September 19.
        </p>
        <p class="text-left">
          Dr. Elaasar will be joined by Dr. Mustafa Hafizoglu, a Project Management Institute (PMI) veteran from
          Turkey, and Dr. Ahmad AlGhamdi, professor of systems engineering at the University of Jeddah. Together they
          bring the program management and systems engineering perspectives that concurrent engineering lives on.
        </p>
        <p class="text-left">
          The session begins with an overview of concurrent engineering: how great teams design complex systems
          together by working in parallel rather than in sequence, integrating early, and making trade-offs visible
          across disciplines.
        </p>
        <p class="text-left">
          Then the theory gets put to the test. In a live simulation, participants step into the role of suppliers to
          a large aircraft OEM, competing to develop paper airplane prototypes. Each team must procure materials,
          design, prototype, and test their proposed concept, then demonstrate it live. Teams have to respect the
          OEM's technical specifications and compete on performance, all within a fixed budget, while issuing press
          releases that keep investors betting on their stock.
        </p>
        <p class="text-left">
          Winners will be declared in each category, followed by a discussion of lessons learned: what the simulation
          reveals about communication, integration, and decision making in real concurrent engineering programs.
        </p>
