Engineering is a team sport, but most modeling tools still assume one expert and one license dongle. OML Code treats collaboration as a core capability, not an add-on.

You get two collaboration modes and use each where it fits. Versioned collaboration follows the pull, edit, push rhythm software teams have trusted for decades: deliberate, reviewable, and auditable. Live collaboration lets teammates edit concurrently when you are working a problem together in real time.

When it is time to review, OML Code gives you two model diff modes. Text-based diffs make precise, line-level changes obvious. Viewpoint-based diffs show what changed in engineering terms, through the same viewpoints your stakeholders already use, so reviewers reason about content rather than syntax.

#### What changes for your team

- Modeling stops being a single-expert bottleneck and becomes a team activity
- Every change is attributable, reviewable, and reversible
- Reviews focus on engineering meaning, with diffs at the right level of abstraction
- Distributed teams work one model without stepping on each other

If your current review process involves screenshots and a long meeting, you will enjoy this demo.
