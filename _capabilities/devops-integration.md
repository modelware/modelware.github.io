Software teams learned long ago that quality comes from pipelines, not heroics. OML Code brings that same protection, agility, and efficiency to engineering knowledge.

Version control is Git-native. Each repository carries a baseline branch, and models federate cleanly across repositories, so programs scale without merging everything into one monolith.

Continuous integration runs on every commit: lint, validate, reason, analyze. Peer review follows the same rhythm: diff, compare, review. A contradiction introduced on Tuesday morning is caught Tuesday morning, by the pipeline, before it reaches anyone downstream.

Continuous delivery publishes knowledge with semantic version tags. Select models into packages, publish them to artifact repositories, feed them to other tools such as read-only databases, render read-only viewpoints as gate products, and post those gate products to your project portal. Reviews stop being scrambles because the deliverables are generated, versioned, and already current.

#### What your program gains

- A protected baseline: nothing lands without passing validation and reasoning
- Full traceability of who changed what, when, and why
- Gate products that assemble themselves from verified knowledge
- Engineering knowledge delivered with the same discipline as released software

This is the capability that turns modeling from a side activity into engineering infrastructure.

Ask us to walk through a real pipeline, from a model edit to a published gate product. It is the demo program managers remember.
